Hyperius Blake

Cleveland-based Multi-instrumentalist, arranger, and producer

Leader of the Sound Experiment Music Collective

Regularly misunderstood, always gaining understanding.

Robin "Hyperius" Blake, a self-professed musical alchemist, captivates audiences with a blend of high artistry, "phat" grooves, and quality sound. Hailing from Cleveland, Ohio, this talented artist has been working on the local scene for a decade as an instrumentalist, vocalist, composer, and organizer.

Hyperius discovered his passion for music at the tender age of 8, when he was gifted an ancestral violin from his parents, paving the way for a distinctive musical journey that led to him obtaining a Bachelor's degree in Music, with a focus on the African diaspora.

Whether producing solo or working with his music collective, the Sound Experiment, he strives to move audiences through compelling storytelling and beautiful composition.

The Sound Experiment

Known for their electrifying stage presence, Hyperius Blake & the Sound Experiment bring their music to life in dynamic live performances. From intimate venues to grand stages, each show is an immersive and passionate experience, leaving audiences captivated by the energy and emotion conveyed through their music.

They acknowledge genre while transcending it at the same time. Influenced by music of the Black Diaspora, they combine the sounds of jazz, hip-hop, neo-soul and world music with finesse, creating something truly unique.

"Worth" is the Sound Experiment's debut single. A soulful and introspective composition, serving as a testament to the artist's personal journey of self-discovery and acceptance. This emotionally charged piece delves into the themes of desire, growth, and self-worth, exploring the challenges of measuring one's success against the achievements of others. The inclusion of horns and strings adds depth and warmth to the composition, creating a sonically immersive experience for the listener. Stacked harmonies and intricate counterpoint contribute to the emotional intensity of the song, allowing the audience to connect with the artist's vulnerability.
