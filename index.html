Biography

A multi-instrumentalist with roots in classical training, this artist's musical journey began at age 8 with the violin. Their passion for the arts flourished throughout their academic years, encompassing theater, musicals, and various band ensembles. Graduating from Cleveland State University in 2016 with a focus on saxophone and voice, they expanded their instrumental repertoire to include clarinet, oboe, and flute. Their senior thesis, exploring the connection between hip-hop and jazz, laid the foundation for the Black Voltron music collective. Since graduation, they've become an integral part of Cleveland's music scene, performing with the Cleveland Orchestra Chorus, various bands, and churches. As founder of "Creation: A Celebration of Art, Music, and Life," they continue to foster community through innovative, cross-genre, multi-disciplnary art experiences.

Artist Statement

I create music that blends high artistry, solid grooves, and complex arrangements, both solo and with my collective, the Sound Experiment. My work fuses modern and classic elements across genres, exploring the human condition and enhancing connection. As a Black, queer, and neurodivergent person, I acknowledge my unique perspective, and celebrate all the sounds of my culture, channeling ancestral strength through song.

Black Voltron

A wise man once said to "play the universe". We're here to show what happens when universes collide.

Formerly known as Hyperius Blake & the Sound Experiment, Black Voltron delivers electrifying live performances, blending classical training, church roots, and diverse lived experiences. Their versatile musicianship captivates audiences in venues of all sizes, conveying raw energy and emotion, and giving "goosebumps". Transcending genre boundaries, they create a unique sound that fuses jazz, disco/house, hip-hop, neo-soul, and world music, honoring the Black Diaspora while innovating contemporary music. When Black Voltron unites, they can play "anything".
